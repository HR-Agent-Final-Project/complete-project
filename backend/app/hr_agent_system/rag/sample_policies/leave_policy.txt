COMPANY LEAVE POLICY — Sri Lanka Operations
============================================

ANNUAL LEAVE (AL)
─────────────────
Entitlement:      14 working days per calendar year
Notice Required:  Minimum 3 working days in advance
Max Continuous:   14 days (longer requires HR Manager approval)
Carry-Over:       Up to 7 unused days may be carried to the next year
Availability:     Not available during probation period (first 6 months)
Attendance Rule:  Employee must maintain above 85% attendance to qualify

SICK LEAVE (SL)
───────────────
Entitlement:      7 working days per calendar year
Medical Cert:     Required for more than 2 consecutive sick days
Carry-Over:       Cannot be carried over to the next year
Notice:           Notify supervisor as early as possible on the day
Doctor Note:      Must be submitted within 48 hours of return

CASUAL LEAVE (CL)
─────────────────
Entitlement:      7 working days per calendar year
Max per Request:  3 consecutive days
Carry-Over:       Cannot be carried over
Notice:           Advance notice required where possible

MATERNITY LEAVE (ML)
────────────────────
Entitlement:      84 working days (12 weeks) paid leave
Notice Required:  At least 4 weeks before expected delivery date
Documents:        Medical certificate confirming expected delivery date
Approval:         Mandatory HR Manager approval
Eligibility:      Permanent employees only (not contractors or probation)
Extension:        Unpaid extension possible with HR Manager approval

PATERNITY LEAVE (PL)
────────────────────
Entitlement:      3 working days
Timing:           Must be taken within 2 weeks of child's birth
Approval:         HR Manager approval required

NO PAY LEAVE (NPL)
──────────────────
Availability:     Only when all paid leave entitlements are exhausted
Deduction:        Full salary deduction for NPL days
Approval:         HR Manager mandatory approval
Maximum:          30 days per calendar year

LEAVE RULES — GENERAL
─────────────────────
- Leave cannot be taken during the notice period of resignation
- Public holidays during a leave period do not count as leave days
- Leave applications must be submitted through the HR system
- Retrospective leave applications require HR Manager approval
- Abandonment of work (absent 3+ days without notice) is a disciplinary offence

ATTENDANCE THRESHOLD
────────────────────
Minimum 85% attendance required per month.
Employees below 85% may have leave applications reviewed more strictly.
Repeated below-threshold months trigger a formal HR review.
